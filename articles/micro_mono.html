Microservices vs Monolithic Architecture

Reference: Microservices vs Monolith Architecture





In this article written by French web developer Alex Barashkov, microservices are compared to monolith architecture. After reading what he wrote, it seems like the microservice route would be the harder but better option given that it’s often more reliable, has no single point of failure, among other benefits. However, the author makes it clear that there are pros and cons to both routes and your team and project will contribute greatly to which will be used.



The comparisons that are made remind me a lot of the arguments made between on-premise architecture and the cloud. Amazon Web Services, for example, uses microservices and offers exceptional scalability and reliability of servers, can be very cost effective, and makes deployments and releases fast. All of these qualities are shared with microservices, as well as not having a single point of failure. But that does not mean AWS is perfect for every problem, company, or project. On-premise architecture allows you to have physical control of servers and be more cost effective if you do not need near-perfect uptime.
